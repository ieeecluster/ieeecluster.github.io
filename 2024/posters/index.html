Call for Posters ● Cluster 2024

IEEE Cluster 2024 is soliciting submissions from academia, laboratory, and industry professionals to present their latest research findings and work-in-progress in all aspects of cluster, cloud, and grid technologies in the form of posters, which will be included in the conference proceedings. The topics of interest are in the areas of 1) Application, Algorithms, and Libraries, 2) Architecture, Network/Communications, and Management, 3) Programming and System Software, and 4) Data, Storage, and Visualization, as in the Cluster 2024 Call for Papers.

Students and young researchers are especially encouraged to submit a poster and present their work in the conference. All submissions will be reviewed by the Posters Program Committee, and the highest-quality student poster and presentation will be awarded with the Best Student Poster Award. Posters undergo a single-blind review process.

Students may be eligible to apply for travel supports, with details on eligibility and application procedures to be announced at https://clustercomp.org/2024/mentoring/.

Accepted posters will be prominently displayed and presented during a session in the conference.

Submissions

Participants must submit a 2-page extended abstract (including references) describing the poster content, and the research ideas in the poster. If accepted, this extended abstract will be published in the proceedings of the conference.

The extended abstract should be structured similar to a technical paper, and may not exceed two letter-sized (8.5" x 11") pages including figures, tables and references using the IEEE format for conference proceedings (PDF, single-spaced, double column, 10pt text). Please submit the extended abstract via the online submission system at https://ssl.linklings.net/conferences/ieeecluster/.

Guidelines for Artificial Intelligence (AI)-Generated Text

The use of content generated by artificial intelligence (AI) in a paper (including but not limited to text, figures, images, and code) shall be disclosed in the acknowledgments section of any paper submitted to an IEEE publication. The AI system used shall be identified, and specific sections of the paper that use AI-generated content shall be identified and accompanied by a brief explanation regarding the level at which the AI system was used to generate the content.

The use of AI systems for editing and grammar enhancement is common practice and, as such, is generally outside the intent of the above policy. In this case, disclosure as noted above is recommended.

Please also refer IEEE Submission Policies
