Topic: Intel Inside Cluster Computing
Speaker: David S. Scott (Intel)

The number of Intel based computers in the TOP500 list has grown been rapidly growing from 3, to 26, to 56, to 119 on June list. This growth has been driven by a number of factors. Moore's law has guaranteed that the individual processors have continued to improve in performance. The availability of the Linux operating system and commercial high performance interconnects has made it cost effective to build large clusters. The increasing availability of tools and application software that have been ported and tuned for clusters has expanded the potential user base beyond computer scientists. This talk will review the current status and future plans of Intel Architecture based clusters.

About speaker

David Scott received his PhD in Numerical Analysis from UC Berkeley in 1978. After working at the Oak Ridge National Laboratory and the University of Texas in Austin, he joined Intel's parallel computing group in 1985. He held a variety of positions including leading the math library team that achieved the first MPLinpack Teraflop on the ASCI Red system. He recently relocated to Singapore to support HPC in the Asia Pacific region.

Topic: Scaling out IT Solutions through Standardization of Hardware and Software
Speaker: Newman Wong (Dell Advanced Systems Group - Asia Pacific)

This talk intends to compare and contrast enterprise computing solutions built using one of the two architectures: Scaling Out: Industry-standard 2-way, 4-way, or 8-way SMPs (Symmetric Multi-Processing), in conjunction with other industry-standard connectivity technologies, such as high speed interconnects. Scaling Up: Larger, monolithic SMP machines, with more than 8 processors. There are many axes along which this comparison may occur, including hardware characteristics, software availability (which covers operating systems, middleware and applications), reliability, availability, and serviceability (RAS) features, administrative and management functions, and price/performance. This talk will offer usage models for scale-out in high performance cluster computing, resource partitioning and allocation, database grids, and high availability to demonstrate our perspective.

About speaker

Newman s a technologist at Dell Advanced Systems Group - Asia Pacific. He is responsible for advancing Dell Oracle and HPCC solutions on Intel architecture, especially the Backend Database Cluster solutions. Most recently Newman's work has focused on designing hardware and systems software infrastructure solutions to support the deployment of High Performance Computing (HPC) applications, using clusters running either the Windows or Linux operating systems.
