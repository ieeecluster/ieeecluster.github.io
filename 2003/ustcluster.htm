Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (UST)

Dr. Ishfaq AHMAD and his colleagues established the Multimedia Technology Research Center (MTREC, http://www.mtrec.ust.hk/). The mission of the Multimedia Technology Research Center is to engage in leading-edge research and development activities for achieving academic excellence and for producing multimedia-based technologies that can support existing as well as emerging multimedia information applications, and to transfer technology to local industries in Hong Kong and the world. A 20-node UltraA1/170 167 MHz workstation cluster has been used to study the scheduling and load balancing in multimedia applications. Recent projects include software based on MPEG for video services, software only Video-phone project, multimedia email system.

Physics PC Cluster Project:

The Physics Department of the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology has developed a supercomputing platform powered by AMD Athlon processors that could enable researchers to perform realistic modeling and simulation for science and engineering problems. Entitled "Physics PC Cluster Project” (http://physics.ust.hk/pccluster/), the purpose of this project is to achieve supercomputing power by using off-the-shelf hardware and public domain software.

The whole cluster consists of 48 compute nodes with AMD Athlon 1.0 GHz CPU, 32 compute nodes with AMD Athlon 1.4GHz CPU and 8 dual CPU compute nodes with AMD Athlon MP 1900+ CPU. The first eighty nodes are equipped with 512MB memory, while the remaining 8 dual mode are equipped with 1GB PC2100 Registered ECC DDR-SDRAM and all nodes have 15-40 GB hard drive and three Intel Pro/100+ Fast Ethernet cards for local data The OS is SuSE Linux 6.4 (kernel 2.4.10-ac12). The multi-NIC configuration helps to build a two-way Fast Ethernet Channel Bonding that potentially can double the communication bandwidth. All PCs are connected by a 3Com 4007 switch with 24Gbps fabric and total 216 100Mbps ports. Best LINPACK benchmark of all 80 nodes is 67.49 GFLOPS, while the theoretical peak is 185.6 GFLOPS. The Physics Department also has several small-scale x86- and Alpha-based Linux Clusters.

Currently the cluster is used for physics research. The areas include: (1) First princple studies of material properties (e.g., atoms absorbed on metal service, band structure calculation) (2) Numerical study of strongly correlated electron systems (e.g. t-J models with two charge carriers).

The HKUST group is also trying to run the Numerical Weather Prediction system operationally at a much finer resolution of 2-6 km on the Linux PC-cluster system. The HKUST's NWP system is a regional system covering Hong Kong and the South China coastal region. Its horizontal resolution is 18 km, much finer than the typical resolution of about 100 km in global models.

On 8 May 2001, HKUST and Advanced Micro Devices Far East Ltd (AMD) signed a memorandum of understanding for cooperation. Both parties will jointly explore and plan for a larger and more advanced 128-node project.
